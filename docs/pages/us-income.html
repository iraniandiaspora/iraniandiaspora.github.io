First-generation Iranian-Americans (ages 25–54) are concentrated in the upper income deciles, with 21% in the top decile—more than double the national baseline of 10%.
Second-generation Iranian-Americans (ages 25–54) are even more concentrated at the top, with 25% in the highest income decile and only 6% in the lowest.

Source: U.S. Census Bureau — ACS 2020–2024 5-Year PUMS
Ages 25–54 (prime working years).
Each decile holds 10% of all U.S. households, ranked by pre-tax household income.
