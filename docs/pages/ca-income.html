First-generation Iranian-Canadian households are concentrated at the bottom of the national income distribution: 19% fall in the lowest decile, nearly double the 10% national baseline. Only 7% reach the top decile. This contrasts with the United States, where first-generation Iranian-Americans are overrepresented at the top.
Among first-generation Iranian-Canadian earners, the share making CA$100,000 or more is highest at ages 35–54. The individual pre-tax income distribution differs from the household income pattern, where Iranian-Canadians are concentrated at the bottom.
